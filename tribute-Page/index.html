Jagadish Chandra Bose

1858 - 1937

Biologist, Physicist, Botanist and an early writer of Science Fiction

The Following is a timeline of his life

  • 1858 - Was born at Mymensingh, now in Bangladesh.
  • 1884 - Completed his B.Sc. degree from Cambridge University.
  • 1884 - Appointed as Professor of Physical Science at Presidency College, Calcutta (now Kolkata).
  • 1917 - Left his professorship and established the Bose Institute at Calcutta which was initially devoted principally to the study of plants.
  • 1901 - Conducted a scientific breakthrough experiment which proved that plants have feelings.
  • 1902 - Authored his first book titled ‘Response in the Living and Non-living’.
  • 1917 - Was Knighted
  • 1920 - Elected as the Fellow of the Royal Society in 1920 for his amazing contributions and achievements
  • 1926 - Authored his second book titled ‘The Nervous Mechanism of Plants’.
  • 1930 - 1935 - Extensively researched the behavior of radio waves. Mostly known as a plant physiologist, he was actually a physicist. Bose made improvements on another instrument called ‘the coherer’, for detecting the radio waves.
  • 1937 - Died at age 78 in Giridih, India.
